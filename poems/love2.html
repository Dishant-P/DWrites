How silly?

How silly we kids are, we would do whatever it takes To hold on to something we adore But how can you capture this thing This feeling? Ah! I wish I can tell this to someone 7th April 2008 The bell rings, and all the kids rush towards the door Yelling the single song we all hold dear She got up too, and so did I As usual, we all sit together, her being opposite to me The lunch gets over. We get up from the desks Removing the dirt from seats caused by our shoes She comes around, eager to ask something She holds my hand and pulls me aside “Holy shit!” I screamed inside my head, “She touched me!” I couldn’t even hear what she was saying With the cotton balls shaped like a palm The blood was squeezed out of my veins My hand turned red, and so did my cheeks, I guess Well, I answered the question that I surprisingly remember Till this date, because this was something special I wanted to lock this touch safely forever But what’s safer than the locker of our memories? Searching for that something, I picked up the pen The words didn’t rhyme, nor were they “too creative” But that feeling is still as pure as it was 13 years ago Something that never felt silly, nor does it now I didn’t know what will happen next nor I gave it much thought All I cared was to store this moment forever That’s it. I was hooked. Moments came, the pages filled themselves That time I wished that I can tell this to someone Never realized That someone can be me too! Cause I did! Today. When I looked for this diary entry After 13 long years, I relived the entire scene back I smiled. I subconsciously touched my forearm again The one she touched. Realized, and laughed again I put the diary on my chest, pages open on my heart Close my eyes, and do it all over again
