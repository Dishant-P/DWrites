Warmth, nostalgia and a torn piece of paper

Today the air was thick and opaque I missed a crack in the pavement and took the knee I asked the town, ‘Why so hard on me today?’ He peevishly replied, ‘it's been a long time, things change’ I was shook, had nowhere to look. I simply replied ‘You’re not wrong’ But no matter how many masks a person may wear, The air still comes from the true self, not veneer No matter how many times you paint a wall with care, The concrete can still be smell when its wet and near No matter how many clothes you put on, as many as you dare, The smell of the body is always revealed with one true whiff, it’s clear Then why this sudden change? I know it’s not rare, Cause we were never meant to be aliens, at least not mere And not just that but the lilies are still there I walked on this path because I always did when I felt sad I again smelled the roses that crossed my way here But I didn’t feel as relieved as I was in the arms of my dad I grew, and blossomed in your garden that you tended But now I can’t even find the place from where I was plucked Before I never felt my feet on the road, I floated Now, every step feels like a step in quicksand, Ah! I out lucked I again missed a crack in the pavement, this time I fell hard I woke up with that angry slap of yours, I looked at the yard Took out my ticket out of here, and I smell your air breezing I tear the ticket, everything’s stitched, you and me blazing
