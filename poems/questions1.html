Quelques ‘bête’ questions

I just turned ‘not a child anymore’ No, it’s not my birthday, my dad is just a bit annoyed with me But wait, I never wanted this Because not knowing things still scare me. I am scared that my bathroom will be the only place I could cry freely, Not being judged by anyone. And the whole world will start moving so fast, that everything would be blurry (Maybe I could try wearing glasses) I won't be in a different house each summer anymore (I loved to spend vacations at my uncles') Now my house will remain the same And maybe just the people will change As if they weren’t changing enough already My ancestors won’t live up in those infinite shining eyes of the sky Now I won't mistake salt for sugar, Fingers for brushes, smiles for happiness, Eyes for mirrors to the soul. But most importantly I won't mistake ‘just colleagues’ for friends I won't ask questions because I will understand that there are questions that are stupid/silly Now I won't just see the outside of people but also the inside and then I will know them, Understand them. And then realize that maybe it’s not always a good thing to do so. I will start smiling less, or maybe only to a mirror Because I will hear what people say about me when they think that I am not listening (Thank god, the mirror doesn’t speak anything behind my back) Because I will know! I will know all these things. Things I am not ready for. But maybe that’s how I will feel from now on. ‘Just not ready’ No, I won’t ask myself ‘why me?’ Will I be that ‘special’ anymore Cause people say each ‘child’ is special, what changes afterward? It feels just like a movie Started a while ago and all good things are done and now all that is left is facing the world and its challenges and more. And we all know, it may not be a happy ending. Because who likes cliches anymore?
